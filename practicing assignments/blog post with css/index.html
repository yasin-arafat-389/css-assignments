On the first day of the lockdown, the pressure of small vehicles in Daulatdia

On the first day of the week-long all-out lockdown announced by the government, private cars, microbuses and small vehicles are under pressure at Daulatdia in Rajbari, one of the entrances to the south-west, from Wednesday morning. The drivers of these vehicles are crowding in front of the designated counter to collect tickets for the ferry crossing.

In the morning, a crowd was seen in front of the counter of Bangladesh Inland Water Transport Corporation (BIWTC). In front of him is a line of small cars including private cars and microbuses. The drivers of these vehicles are crowding to collect ferry tickets. At that time most of the drivers did not have masks on their faces. Hygiene rules were not obeyed. Most of the drivers are scattered in front of the counter without masks. Many expressed frustration at not getting tickets. Most of the ferries at the wharf are sitting on the ramp. Many are going back as they cannot cross the river. There are some cargo cars sitting in the terminal.

Egypt will not leave the ship unless it pays 900 million

Egypt seizes Panama-flagged ship stranded in Suez Canal An Egyptian court has fined the ship's owner 900 million in damages for blocking important waterways and depriving the country of revenue. If this money is not paid, the ship weighing two lakh tons will not be returned. Suez Canal authorities reported the matter on Wednesday. Indian media reported on NDTV.

Last month, the ship 'Ever Given' got stuck almost horizontally. The Suez Canal was closed to traffic. The two entrances to the Suez Canal are congested. The container ship was able to move after about a week of continuous efforts through various integrated techniques. As a result, the Suez Canal was reopened.
